Hi. I’m Wolf. I use mathematical models to ask questions about intelligence & sustainability. Sometimes I answer them.

Research

Collective action is required to enter sustainable development pathways in coupled social-ecological systems, safely away from dangerous tipping elements. The challenge is how such collective action can be successfully organized, in a system of multiple, autonomous and adaptive actors. Yet, multi-agent systems, in which agents interact in a shared environment with possibly conflicting goals, lack a proper theoretical grounding.

My research addresses this challenge in a two-step procedure.

  1. Collective Learning Dynamics
    First, I develop a better understanding of systems, composed of multiple self-learning agents by using methods from dynamical systems theory to describe the dynamics of collective learning.
  2. Mathematics of Sustainability
    Second, I apply these theoretical insights to synthesize solutions principles for societal collective action challenges such as sustainable development.

I work with multi-agent systems, reinforcement learning, game theory, and dynamical systems, among other tools.
